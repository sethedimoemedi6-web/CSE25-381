Crypto Frequently Asked Questoins

  1. What is cryptocurrency?

    Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency secured by cryptography, making it nearly impossible to counterfeit. Unlike traditional fiat currencies (like the US Dollar), most cryptocurrencies are decentralized networks based on blockchain technology, a distributed ledger enforced by a disparate network of computers

  2. What is Bitcoin?

    Bitcoin is the first and most widely adopted cryptocurrency, created in 2009. It operates as a peer-to-peer electronic cash system, allowing users to send, receive, and store value without intermediate financial institutions

  3. What is Blockchain?

    A blockchain is a decentralized, distributed, and public digital ledger used to record transactions across many computers so that the record cannot be altered retroactively.

  4. How do I keep my cryptocurrency safe?

    Use Cold Storage: Use hardware wallets (offline devices) for long-term storage. Enable 2FA: Activate two-factor authentication (2FA) on all exchange accounts. Protect Seed Phrases: Never share your seed phrase (private keys) with anyone, and store it offline.

  5. How are cryptocurrency prices determined?

    Crypto prices are driven by supply and demand, influenced by market sentiment, adoption rates, regulatory news, and macroeconomic factors.

  6. What is a "wallet" in crypto?

    A crypto wallet is a software program or hardware device that allows users to store, send, and receive digital assets. It stores the private keys needed to authorize transactions on the blockchain.

  7. What is a central exchange (CEX) vs. decentralized exchange (DEX)?

    CEX (e.g., Coinbase, Binance): A centralized platform that acts as an intermediary, holding your funds and managing security. DEX (e.g., Uniswap): A platform that allows peer-to-peer trading directly from your own wallet without an intermediary

  8. What is "staking"?

    Staking is the process of locking up crypto assets to help verify transactions on a Proof-of-Stake (PoS) blockchain. In return, participants earn rewards, similar to earning interest in a bank account.

  9. What is a Stablecoin?

    A stablecoin is a type of cryptocurrency designed to have a stable price, usually pegged to a fiat currency like the US Dollar (e.g., USDT, USDC).

  10. What are the main risks of investing in crypto?

    Key risks include high price volatility, potential for security breaches (hacks/scams), regulatory uncertainty, and the risk of losing access to private keys, which leads to permanent loss of funds

  11. How can I start buying cryptocurrency?

    You can buy cryptocurrency on reputable exchanges by creating an account, verifying your identity (KYC), linking a bank account or credit card, and placing an order.
