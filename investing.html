Trading and investing strategies 1:Day trading and swing trading

Day TradingDay trading means opening and closing positions within the same day to capture short-term price moves.

Key points:

  • Trade on short timeframes (minutes–hours), use limit and market orders, and follow strict stop-loss rules to protect capital.
  • Day trading requires fast decision-making, constant screen time, and tight risk controls

Swing Trading

Swing trading Swing trading holds positions for several days to a few weeks to capture medium-term trends.

Key points:

  • focus on trend direction, use daily and 4‑hour charts, set entry/exit rules, and size positions so a single loss won’t hurt your portfolio.
  • Swing trading suits traders who cannot monitor markets every hour but can check daily.

Trading and investing strategies 2:Scalping and buy and hold

Scalping Scalping targets very small price moves with many trades per day

Key points:

  • very short holding times (seconds–minutes), high trade frequency, low per-trade risk, and tight spreads/fees are critical
  • holding times (seconds–minutes), high trade frequency, low per-trade risk, and tight spreads/fees are critical

Buy AND Hold

Buy and hold means purchasing crypto and keeping it long term, ignoring short-term volatility.

Key points:

  • Focus on fundamentals, diversify across projects, use secure custody (hardware wallets), and rebalance periodically
  • This strategy reduces time spent trading and can lower tax/fee drag.

Understanding technical indicators

What they are Technical indicators are mathematical calculations applied to price and volume to help identify trends, momentum, and potential reversals. Common beginner indicators: Moving Averages (trend), RSI (momentum/overbought), MACD (trend + momentum), and Volume (confirmation). Use indicators as tools, not guarantees.

How to use them

  1. Combine indicators (trend + momentum) to reduce false signals.
  2. Backtest simple rules on historical data before risking real funds.
  3. Keep charts uncluttered: 2–3 indicators are usually enough for beginners.

Building a well balanced crypto portfolio

Core Principles

  1. - Diversify across large-cap (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum) and a few smaller projects. /li>
